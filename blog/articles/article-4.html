Efficiency Often Begins in the Smallest, Most Repetitive Tasks

效率的起點，往往藏在最瑣碎的流程裡

Do we really need to spend hours just moving numbers from PDFs to Excel?

我們真的需要花幾個小時，只為了把數字從 PDF 搬到 Excel 嗎？

In many companies, handling invoices is still a tedious and time-consuming job. Each PDF has to be opened manually, the date and amount copied into Excel or Google Sheets, and then checked again and again. A handful of invoices might be manageable. But when the stack grows to dozens or even hundreds, finance staff can spend an entire day just moving numbers from one place to another.

The first time I encountered this, I was surprised. Even in companies that called themselves "digital," invoice processing was still stuck at the stage of manual copy-and-paste. It was slow, error-prone, and fragile - a single digit typed wrong could throw off an entire report, sometimes even affecting decisions.

At its core, the problem was simple: this is a low-value repetitive task. Each PDF looks roughly the same. The same information gets copied every time. And with every new invoice, the whole routine has to start over. Meanwhile, the work that really matters - financial analysis, planning, strategy - keeps getting pushed to the side.

Later, I tried building a small automation with n8n. The idea was straightforward: once an invoice is dropped into a folder, the system reads the file, extracts the key details like date and amount, and writes them directly into Google Sheets. What used to take 3 - 4 hours could now be done in just a few minutes.

The impact wasn't only about saving time. It also meant finance teams could get real-time data faster and focus their attention where it truly adds value - analysis and decision-making.

This convinced me even more: efficiency gains don't always come from expensive ERP systems or massive IT projects. The real starting point is often the smallest, most repetitive workflows. Once these tasks are automated, companies can free up energy to focus on the work that actually moves them forward.

在許多公司，處理發票依舊是一件瑣碎又耗時的工作。每一張 PDF 都要人工打開、複製日期和金額、貼到 Excel 或 Google Sheets，最後還得反覆檢查。當發票數量只有幾張時或許還好，但累積到幾十、上百張時，財務人員常常需要花上一整天。

我第一次遇到這類工作時很驚訝：即使在「數位化」的公司，發票處理仍然停留在人工搬運的階段。除了耗時，錯誤也難以避免：一個小小的數字輸入錯誤，就可能讓整份財務報表對不上，甚至影響決策。

問題的核心很單純：這是一種低附加價值的重複性任務。每份 PDF 格式大同小異，每次都要做一樣的複製貼上，每來一張新發票，就得再跑一遍。真正能帶來價值的工作，像是財務分析或預算規劃，反而被擠到後面。

後來我嘗試用 n8n 做了一個小小的自動化流程。概念很簡單：發票一放進資料夾，系統就會自動讀取、擷取日期與金額，然後寫進 Google Sheets。過去需要 3 - 4 小時的工作，現在幾分鐘就能完成。

這樣的改變帶來的不只是省下時間，而是讓財務人員更快拿到即時數據，把心力用在分析與決策上。

這讓我更確信一件事：效率的提升，不一定要靠昂貴的 ERP 系統或大型專案。真正的起點，往往是那些最日常、最瑣碎的流程。當這些任務被自動化後，企業才能把能量釋放到更有價值的地方。
