My Notes 我的筆記 September 2, 2025

Sapiens

《人類大歷史》

Humanity's strength comes from collective imagination.

人類的強大來自「集體想像」。

When I read Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind, one idea stood out to me: humanity's strength comes from collective imagination.

Nations, companies, money, and laws don't exist in nature. They only work because we believe in them together. These shared beliefs allow strangers to cooperate on a large scale and move civilization forward.

Another surprising insight was about unintended consequences. The Agricultural Revolution looked like progress, but it actually made daily life harder. In the same way, software made data processing faster, but also created more tasks for us. Efficiency doesn't always mean happiness. History often works like a trade-off: every gain comes with a loss.

What amazed me even more is how little humans have changed. Seventy thousand years ago, Homo sapiens had the same brain capacity and emotional needs we have today. What changed were the systems around us - from tribal myths to national institutions, from stone tools to artificial intelligence. Our core desires stayed the same; only the things we believe in and depend on have shifted.

This made me think about AI and automation. They are not just tools, but also a new kind of collective imagination. Once people broadly trust AI to process information and support decisions, it becomes a system that shapes how resources are used and how collaboration happens.

The real value of AI is not just faster output, but in how we choose to apply it. When used in daily workflows, AI can automate repetitive tasks and free people to focus on higher-value work like analysis and creation. The main question isn't about AI itself, but whether we can design the right systems that turn it into a true driver of growth.

History is not only about the past - it's a mirror. By seeing how collective imagination and trade-offs shaped today, we can make better choices for tomorrow.

讀《人類大歷史》時，我最震撼的一個觀點是：人類的強大來自「集體想像」。

國家、公司、金錢、法律，這些並不存在於自然界，卻因為我們共同相信，它們才得以運作。這種虛構的秩序，讓陌生人能大規模合作，推動文明前進。

另一個顛覆的地方是「意料之外的後果」。農業革命看似進步，卻讓人生活更辛苦；軟體出現後，處理數據的速度加快，但同時也讓每個人的日常任務暴增。這提醒我，效率與幸福並不劃上等號。歷史往往是一種交換條件：得到什麼，也會失去什麼。

更令人驚訝的是，七萬年前的智人和今天的我們，其實差不多。腦容量、情感需求相同，只是外部系統不斷演化——從部落神話到國家制度，從石器到人工智慧。人類的欲望沒有太大改變，變的是我們選擇相信什麼、依靠什麼。

想到這裡，我自然聯想到 AI 與自動化。它們並不只是工具，而是一種新的「集體想像」：當我們普遍信任 AI 能處理資訊、協助決策，它就會成為新的制度，影響資源分配與合作方式。

AI 的價值，不在於單純加快產出，而在於如何設計使用方式。當它被導入到日常流程中，重複性的任務可以被自動處理，人的時間則能專注在更高價值的分析與創造。真正的關鍵，不是 AI 本身，而是我們能否打造出正確的系統，讓它成為推動成長的力量。

歷史不是只是過去，而是一面鏡子。當我們看清「集體想像」與「意外後果」如何塑造今天，就能更有意識地選擇。
